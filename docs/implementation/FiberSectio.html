FiberSection

The fiber section model implements a complex cross section built from multiple FiberMaterial material class instances

\[\varepsilon(z) = \varepsilon_0 - z \,\phi\]
\[f = \int_A \sigma(z)\, dA \qquad \qquad M = -\int_A z\sigma(z)\, dA\]
\[df = \int_A \mathbb{C}(z)\, dA \, d\varepsilon_0 -\int_A z\mathbb{C}(z)\, dA \, d\phi\]
\[dM = -\int_A z\mathbb{C}(z)\, dA \, d\varepsilon_0 +\int_A z^2\mathbb{C}(z)\, dA \, d\phi\]

where

\(\varepsilon_0\)

axial strain (input)

\(\phi\)

curvature change (input)

\(f\)

resulting axial force (output)

\(M\)

resulting moment (output)

\(EA\)

axial stiffness; constant (input parameters E and A)

\(EI\)

flexural stiffness; constant (input parameters E and I)
